Starting a Website

written by Dene Hoffman on 2023-01-24

"The secret of getting ahead is getting started. The secret of getting started is breaking your complex overwhelming tasks into small manageable tasks, and starting on the first one."

–Mark Twain

As the internet being used at an ever-increasing frequency, so grows the demand for a personal website—a central location for all things associated with a particular individual. As a graduate student, I've seen many of my friends make personal websites to highlight their projects, research interests, hobbies, and more, and the creation of a website to host a CV, publications, and links to research is becoming a common recommendation from potential future employers (or people who might want to ask to you present your work at their conferences). At this time, I'm not looking for new employment, as I still likely have over a year left on my dissertation, but I feel it is likely a good thing to get ahead of it. I won't make any promises (to potential readers or myself) about regular posts, but I hope to update the contents of this site over the next week or so to at least include my current CV and publications.

On the topic of website design, this website was made from a template which in turn uses Lektor for content management. It all interfaces nicely with GitHub Pages, so if anyone is interested in using my site as inspiration, feel free to follow those links and learn more.
